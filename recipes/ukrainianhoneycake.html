Ukrainian honey cake

A Ukrainian honey cake, also sometimes known as medovik, features thin layers of honey-flavored cake made from a thin dough, which is rolled out into circles and baked individually until golden. Each layer is coated with a sour cream frosting, stacked and then allowed to rest overnight, softening the cake. The exterior is then covered with more sour cream frosting and the baked and crushed scraps from the layers. My family are in part Ukrainian I was recently asked to make a cake in aid of a fundraiser to support the people effected by the war in Ukraine, this cake took a lot of practice but its one of my favorites now.

Cake Ingredients

  • 3 tablespoon honey
  • ½ cup + 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 ½ tablespoon butter (25g)
  • 2 eggs
  • ¾ teaspoon baking soda
  • ¾ teaspoon kosher salt
  • 2 cups flour (260g)

Frosting Ingredients

  • ¾ cup heavy cream
  • 24 oz sour cream
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla
  • 1 ½ cup powdered sugar

Get Ahead

  1. Prepare cake layers and frosting in advance; the cake needs to sit overnight before serving.
  2. Prepare a strip of acetate (3 inches wide and 20 inches long) inside an adjustable cake ring cutter or 6-inch ring cutter. Tape the ends to make a ring.
  3. Prepare two sheet pans with silicone baking mats or parchment paper.

Make the Cake Layers

  1. Combine honey, sugar, and butter in a saucepan over low heat until melted, about 5-10 minutes.
  2. Preheat the oven to 350°F.
  3. Remove from heat and stream in beaten eggs while whisking. Add baking soda and salt.
  4. Add flour gradually until a dough forms; divide into 8 pieces.
  5. Roll out each piece on a floured surface and use a 6-inch cutter to punch out circles. Bake for 3-5 minutes until golden.
  6. Cool circles on a rack and bake scraps until golden. Crush for garnish.

Make the Frosting

  1. Whip heavy cream until stiff peaks form.
  2. Whisk sour cream, vanilla, and powdered sugar. Fold in whipped cream.

Assemble the Cake

  1. Place frosting in the mold, add a cake layer, and top with more frosting. Repeat with remaining layers.
  2. Chill overnight for a better structure.
  3. Unmold, frost the sides, and garnish with cake crumbs. Slice and enjoy.
